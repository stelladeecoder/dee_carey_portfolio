Credit card fraud detection (GBM)

Machine learning · Finance risk

Stack: Python, Pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Kaggle

Identifying unusual spending behavior so fraud teams can focus on the transactions that matter most.

Problem

Fraud analysts review large volumes of transactions, but only a fraction truly need closer attention. Without support, teams lose time on routine cases while higher risk activity can slip through.

Approach

I explored cardholder spending patterns to understand what typical behavior looks like, then built a data-driven scoring approach that highlights transactions that stand out from those baselines. The goal was a clear, repeatable process that helps analysts decide where to look first.

Outcome

The result is a lightweight decision-support framework that reduces unnecessary manual review and gives fraud teams a faster way to spot transactions that deserve deeper investigation.

CharityML finding donors

Supervised learning · Donation targeting

Stack: Python, NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, scikit-learn

Helping a charity focus its outreach on people who are more likely to donate.

Problem

Approach

Analyzing TV data – Super Bowl

Sports analytics · Trend analysis

Stack: Python, NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib

Exploring how game results, TV ratings, and halftime shows shape the Super Bowl as a media event.

Problem

The Super Bowl is both a championship game and a major broadcast event, but information about scores, viewership, and halftime performances is rarely viewed together. That makes it hard to see long-term patterns.

Approach

I combined game outcomes, TV ratings, and halftime performer data to study how the event evolves across seasons. I looked at which matchups drew the largest audiences, how ad costs and scoring changed, and how halftime shows aligned with viewership trends.

Outcome

The analysis highlights how entertainment, competition, and audience behavior all interact during the Super Bowl. These patterns can support decisions for brands, media teams, or analysts who plan around major sporting events.

Analyze international debt statistics

Global development · Data exploration

Stack: Python, Pandas, SQL, Jupyter Notebook

Turning World Bank debt figures into a clearer picture of how obligations are distributed across countries.

Problem

Developing countries carry significant debt, but raw tables make it difficult to compare nations or see where obligations are concentrated. Stakeholders need a clearer view of the landscape.

Approach

I worked with World Bank data to summarize total debt by country, compare key categories, and identify which indicators contribute most to overall obligations. The focus was on patterns and relationships rather than isolated figures.

Outcome

The project offers a simplified view of a complex topic. It helps decision-makers quickly see which countries hold the highest debt levels, how categories differ, and where financial risk is most concentrated.

Investigating Netflix movies

Entertainment data · Exploratory analysis

Stack: Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Jupyter Notebook

Studying how Netflix’s movie catalog is changing over time by genre, release year, and runtime.

Problem

With so many titles on Netflix, it is difficult to see how the movie catalog is shifting over time. Differences in runtime, release year, and genre are not obvious from browsing alone.

Approach

I explored Netflix movie data to examine trends in runtimes, the mix of genres, and the timing of releases. The goal was to understand broad content patterns instead of focusing on individual films.

Outcome

The analysis shows how Netflix’s movie library is evolving, from changes in popular genres to shifts in typical runtimes. These insights can support content strategy, recommendation work, or research into viewing habits.
